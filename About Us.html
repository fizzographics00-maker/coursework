ABOUT US


Who we are

MindSpace organisation connects nations, partners and people to promote health, support emotional well-being, keep the world safe and serve the vulnerable so everyone everywhere can attain the highest level of health.


Our goals


  1. Provide stabilization: Offer immediate and intensive care to stabilize individuals with severe symptoms who may be a risk to themselves or others.

  2. Promote recovery: Support individuals in improving their overall mental health and quality of life, aiming for long-term well-being.

  3. Prevent future crises: Promote recovery and implement strategies to prevent the recurrence of severe episodes.

  4. Reduce mental illness incidence: Work to prevent the development of mental disorders through promotion, prevention, and early intervention strategies.

  5. Promote rights and dignity: Ensure that the rights and privacy of individuals are respected and protected from harm.

  6. Reduce stigma: Actively work to reduce the social stigma associated with mental health problems.

Our Patient's Recovery and Success stories


Claire's Story

Many narratives focus on individuals' journeys through illness and their paths to recovery or successful management of their condition. These stories aim to provide hope and serve as inspiration for others facing similar challenges.For instance, Claire's" Story: Deceit and a Mental Health Crisis A woman, identified as Claire, describes how her relationship with a narcissist, filled with cheating and deceit, led to a severe mental and physical decline. She was repeatedly told by her partner that she was the problem when her "paranoia" escalated due to his suspicious behavior. The emotional toll of constantly questioning her own sanity and dealing with his lies resulted in her becoming "a shell of a person," losing weight, experiencing hair loss, and developing red welts on her face. A casting director even told her agent they wouldn't audition her until she got better. The relationship culminated in her partner texting her best friend to say, "Take care of Claire. She's crazy." She describes feeling like a drug addict in withdrawal when without him, highlighting the addictive nature of love chemicals. This experience ultimately led her to seek therapy and later become a heartbreak coach, learning to find her own happiness and trust her instincts.


Zion's Story

In November 2024, my sister went missing and her story went nationwide on the news. Not shortly after, I lost my dad to suicide. It was sudden. It was earth-shattering. And it was the kind of loss that doesn't just break your heart, it breaks your understanding of life. Everything slowed down. The noise of the world faded, and what was left was this quiet, heavy kind of stillness.I started noticing things differently, how people say “family is everything” but drift apart anyway, how we postpone “I love you” for another day that doesn't always come. Grief stripped everything down to what was real. It forced me to face my own mental health, my depression, anxiety, and all the things I used to push aside because they were too uncomfortable to sit with. For a while, I didn't know where to put all that pain. But eventually, I realized I could either let it consume me,or I could build something from it.
