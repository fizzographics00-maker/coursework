SERVICES

We provide a wide range of services including therapy, medication management, crisis intervention, and support groups. Other services include case management, rehabilitation, and educational resources for individuals and families. They operate through various settings such as outpatient clinics, residential facilities, and community-based programs.

Therapy Services

Clinical and support services


Therapy and counseling: Offering various forms of talking therapies like cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) and addiction counseling, which can be face-to-face, online, or via phone.


Medication support: Providing psychiatric medication as a standalone treatment or in combination with therapy.


Direct patient care: Includes consultations with doctors, nurses, and specialists, as well as treatment for acute and chronic illnesses.


Specialty care: Offers services in specific areas like dental clinics, and apheresis units.


Wellness programs: Provide health assessments, nutritional counseling, and physical therapy.



Community and rehabilitation services


Outpatient and day programs:Operating outpatient clinics, day hospitals, and community mental health centers.


Residential care: Offering overnight care and rehabilitation in residential treatment facilities.


Home visits: Providing support and care through home visits from mental health teams.


Rehabilitation: Assisting individuals in recovering from mental health issues through various programs.





Educational services


Educational resources: Offering online guides, apps, and other resources on mental health and how to support others.


Referrals: Connecting individuals with other necessary services, such as social care, housing, and education.


Advocacy: Working to reduce stigma, advocate for patients' rights, and improve the mental health system.



Medication services


The process is a collaborative effort between the patient and the provider to ensure medications are working effectively and safely. Steps generally include:

Initial Assessment: A thorough evaluation of symptoms, medical history, and overall needs to determine if medication is appropriate.


Prescription: If needed, an appropriate medication and dosage are prescribed.


Monitoring and Follow-up: Regular visits are scheduled to monitor the medication's effectiveness, discuss any side effects, and make necessary adjustments to the dosage or type of medication.


Patient Education: Patients receive information on how to take their medication, potential side effects, and what to do if a dose is missed.



Therapy services


Mental health therapy services include various forms like individual, group, and family therapy, as well as specific approaches such as Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) and Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT) and the types of therapies include;

Individual Therapy: One-on-one sessions with a therapist.


Group Therapy: A group of individuals meets with a professional to share experiences and support one another.


Family Counseling: Sessions focus on family dynamics and relationships.


Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT): A therapy that helps identify and change negative thought patterns.


Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT): Provides cognitive and behavioral tools to manage difficult emotions and behaviors.


Psychotherapy: A broader term for a range of talk therapy techniques to help with coping skills.
